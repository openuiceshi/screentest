A blank blue screen (#0000FF) is effective for detecting dead pixels (which appear black) and identifying stuck pixels, particularly those locked on red or green that stand out clearly against the blue background.

Technical Details: The Color Blue

  • Hex Code: #0000FF This is pure digital blue (RGB: 0, 0, 255). It activates the blue sub-pixels at maximum brightness while keeping red and green off.
  • Blue Light Warning Blue light has high energy and can suppress melatonin, affecting your sleep cycle. It is recommended to avoid using full blue screens in a dark room right before bed.

Blue Screen FAQs

Blue vs. Green Screen: Which is better?

Green screens are brighter and better for digital cameras, but blue screens are preferred for darker scenes or if the subject has green eyes/clothing.

Is this the "Blue Screen of Death" (BSOD)?

No. This is a simple blue color tool for testing. The Windows BSOD is a system error screen with text, whereas this is a pure, blank blue display.
